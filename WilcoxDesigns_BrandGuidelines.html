05 · Voice and Tone
How WilcoxDesigns Speaks
Personality
Refined. Quiet confidence.
WilcoxDesigns does not shout. It demonstrates. Work speaks. The brand voice mirrors this: precise, warm, authoritative without arrogance.
Tone in Copy
Precision over performance.
No buzzwords. No trend-chasing language. Direct sentences. Active voice. Every word earns its place on the page.
Client Communication
Collaborative. Clear. Considered.
Clients are treated as partners. Briefs are taken seriously. Feedback is welcomed without defensiveness. Deliverables are explained, not just handed over.
What We Never Do
Compromise the standard.
WilcoxDesigns does not race to the bottom on price, quality, or craft. The brand exists to demonstrate what thoughtful design looks like. This must show in every communication.

07 · Do and Do Not
Usage Rules
Do
  • Use Bebas Neue for all display text and section headings
  • Use DM Sans at weights 300, 400, 500, or 600 only
  • Use Playfair Display italic for taglines and accent moments only
  • Keep the palette warm and restrained. Five colours, used with purpose
  • Lead with craft. Show work. Let quality speak first
  • Maintain generous whitespace. Breathing room is part of the brand
  • Refer to the studio as WilcoxDesigns, one word, capital W and D
Do Not
  • Do not use bright or saturated colours from other brand tiers
  • Do not use PhoennixAI Fire Orange or Electric Blue in WilcoxDesigns materials
  • Do not use Playfair Display in regular weight or non-italic form
  • Do not clutter layouts. Restraint is the aesthetic
  • Do not use aggressive sales language or urgency tactics
  • Do not refer to the studio as Wilcox Designs, two words
  • Do not use em dashes in any written output
08 · UI Components and Status System
Buttons, Chips, and Status Indicators

All interactive elements and document status indicators must follow this system. Consistency across documents, apps, and contractor handouts is non-negotiable. These components apply to the WilcoxDesigns Creative Lab Tool, client documents, and all brand touchpoints.

Buttons, Primary Actions
Confirm Request Update Cancel
Open Document Remove
Primary: Charcoal fill, Linen text. Secondary: Taupe border, no fill. Ghost: Rule border, muted text. Mocha: warm accent actions. Destructive: red border only, reserved for delete or remove actions.
Button Sizes
Large Action Default Small
Large: 12px 24px padding. Default: 8px 16px. Small: 5px 10px. Font always DM Mono, uppercase, letter-spacing 2px. Never use decorative fonts on buttons.
Document Status Chips
System LockedBrand, colour, and type systems. Cannot be changed without a formal review.
ApprovedDocument reviewed and signed off by Valerie Wilcox.
LiveActive and deployed. Available at its permanent URL.
In BuildCurrently being built this session. Not yet approved.
PlannedScoped and in backlog. Build not yet started.
Date TBCAction required. Date or decision outstanding.
ArchivedSuperseded. Kept for audit trail. Not in active use.
Status Indicators, Inline Text
Active System running and operational
In Build Currently in development this session
Pending Action or decision outstanding
Planned Scoped, not yet started
Archived Superseded, kept for audit
Use dots for operational status in dashboards and logs. Use chips for document and deliverable status in cards and tables. Never mix the two in the same row.
Do
  • Use DM Mono for all button labels, chip text, and status indicators
  • Keep button labels to two or three words maximum
  • Use chips for document status, dots for operational status
  • Pair a Primary button with a Ghost or Secondary, never two Primaries
  • Use Mocha chip for approved states, green chip for live states only
Do Not
  • Do not use PhoennixAI Fire Orange or Electric Blue in WilcoxDesigns UI
  • Do not use sentence case on buttons. Always uppercase, always DM Mono
  • Do not create new chip colours outside this system without a brand review
  • Do not use the Destructive button style for anything other than delete actions
  • Do not stack more than three buttons in a single action row
