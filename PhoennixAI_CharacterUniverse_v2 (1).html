THE REAL BATTLE
What They
Actually Fight
They do not fight villains with weapons. They fight something far more insidious. The things that live in the mind and heart of every person who could rise but has not yet.
FEAR
"I am not good with computers." The belief that technology belongs to a certain kind of person and that person is not you. Fear disguises itself as humility but it is actually theft. It steals potential before it has a chance to grow. The team fights fear with evidence. With presence. With "look, you just did it." (2 Timothy 1:7)
LAZINESS
"I cannot be bothered." Not always apathy. Sometimes exhaustion. Sometimes overwhelm dressed up as indifference. The team fights laziness by making the first step so easy and so rewarding that taking it feels better than staying still.
OVERWHELM
"I do not know where to start." The most common villain. The one that stops the most capable people. They can see the mountain but cannot see the first step. The team fights overwhelm with simplicity. One thing at a time. Nobody is handed the whole staircase. Just the next step.
RELIGIOUS FEAR
"AI is evil." This villain wears the face of faith but carries the spirit of confusion. The team does not dismiss this. They honour it. And then they reframe it. A hammer is not evil. AI is not evil. What matters is the heart of the person wielding it. The King gave this generation AI to do good. Always. (2 Timothy 1:7)
PRIDE
"I have done it this way for twenty years. I do not need to change." The quiet killer. The business that was thriving five years ago and is silent today. Not because the owner was not capable. But because they were too proud to adapt. The team fights pride with grace. They never humiliate. They simply show what is possible.
"For God has not given us a spirit of fear, but of power and of love and of a sound mind."
— 2 Timothy 1:7
