06 — Agent Infrastructure Decision
3rd Party vs Build Your Own

The question: should you use 3rd party AI (Claude, Kimi, Manus) or build your own AI models?

The honest answer for where you are right now: Use 3rd party. Do not build your own. Not yet.

Here is why:

Building your own AI model from scratch requires millions in compute costs, a team of ML engineers, months of training time, and terabytes of data. OpenAI spent over $100 million training GPT-4. Anthropic spent similar amounts on Claude. You are a solo founder with two remaining team members. That is not the fight to pick right now.

What you should do instead:

Use Claude, Kimi, and Manus as your agent infrastructure. Combine them intelligently. Build a routing layer that sends the right task to the right model. That gives you the power of all three without the cost of building any.

How to Combine Claude, Kimi, and Manus Right Now

Yes, this is possible. Yes, it is ethical. Yes, you should do it.

Here is how the best AI products in the world work: they do not use one model. They use many. Perplexity uses Claude, GPT, and their own retrieval system. Cursor uses Claude and GPT. You can do the same.

Claude (Anthropic)
Use for: Strategic reasoning, planning, research, writing, complex decision-making, customer-facing conversations. High-quality output. Fast. Reliable. This is me. Use me for anything requiring deep reasoning or brand voice consistency.
Kimi (Moonshot AI)
Use for: Deep research, bulk tasks, cost-effective operations, coding-heavy work. Kimi is open-source, low-cost, and performs nearly as well as Claude on technical tasks. Use Kimi for background processing where cost matters more than speed.
Manus (Autonomous Agent)
Use for: End-to-end autonomous execution. Multi-step workflows. File creation, browsing, system actions without human oversight. Manus is the "virtual employee" that works in a secure container. Use Manus for tasks that require action, not just advice.

How to combine them now (practical steps):

In Phase 0, you manually decide which model to use for which task. ARIA briefings? Claude. Bulk research? Kimi. Autonomous file creation? Manus. Simple routing in your head.

In Phase 2, you build a PhoennixAI orchestration layer. A simple backend service that receives a task, analyses what kind of task it is, and routes it to the right model automatically. User never sees the complexity. They just see PhoennixAI getting smarter.

Example flow:

User asks ARIA: "Brief me on today's tasks." → Routed to Claude (reasoning required).

User asks PULSE: "Create 10 social posts." → Routed to Kimi (bulk content generation, cost matters).

User asks GRID: "Generate a project timeline and create the files." → Routed to Manus (autonomous execution required).

This is not unethical. This is smart architecture. Every major AI product does this.

Important Note on Chinese AI in Europe

You asked why we do not have Chinese AI in Europe. The answer: data sovereignty laws. DeepSeek, Kimi, and other Chinese models store all user data in China, where local laws mandate organisations to share data with intelligence officials upon request. Italy, Germany, and other EU countries have restricted or banned them for this reason.

However: You building in the UK under GDPR is not a limitation. It is your competitive advantage. Your users' data stays in Europe, under European law. That is a selling point, not a weakness.
