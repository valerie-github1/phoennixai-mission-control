05 — About Flyer Links
Do the Flyers Need Clickable Links?

Short answer: No, do not add clickable links to the flyer images themselves.

Here is why:

Social media platforms (especially Instagram) suppress posts with external links in the image or caption because they want to keep users on their platform. If you put a link in the flyer or caption, the algorithm will show it to fewer people. Your reach drops immediately.

What to do instead:

Post the flyer as an image with this caption structure:

Example Instagram Caption for Flyer 5
AI is not here to replace your team. I spent 17 years in the NHS as a Senior Biomedical Scientist. Quality Manager across three hospitals. At 40, Lupus forced me to step back. I could not sustain the demands anymore. So I made a decision. I pivoted into tech. Within two years I became a Business Analyst, a Software Engineer, an AI Engineer, a UX and UI Designer, and a founder. I am also a single mum of three. I built PhoennixAI because I needed it. I was drowning. Now I am not. Beta opens 30 March. Only 10 spots. Drop your name and email in the comments or DM us and we will add you to the waitlist. Rise. Automate. Dominate. #AI #Automation #Founders #SmallBusiness #PhoennixAI

Where the "link" actually is:

  • In your Instagram bio: www.phoennixai.com (already there)
  • People who are interested will click your profile and find the link
  • Or they will DM you directly (which is better because you can start a conversation)

For LinkedIn and X: You can include the website link in the caption on those platforms because their algorithms are more link-friendly. But on Instagram, rely on "link in bio" and DMs.

Best Practice for Beta Signups

The goal is not to send people to a signup form. The goal is to start a conversation. When someone DMs you or comments their email, you capture their details in the spreadsheet AND you send them the welcome message. This builds a relationship before they ever see the product. That is how you turn beta testers into advocates.
