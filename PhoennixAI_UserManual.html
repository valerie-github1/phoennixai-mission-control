SCRIBE Governance
Document Control
SCRIBE's seven-step document control process. Every document in the PhoennixAI ecosystem follows this process without exception.
01
Document Reference Code. Every document receives a unique reference code on creation. Format: PAI-[TYPE]-[NUMBER]. Example: PAI-JD-009 for GRID's Job Description. Types include JD, CV, BG (Brand Guidelines), PRD, LOG, MAN (Manual), BRIEF.
02
Version Number. Every document starts at v1.0. Every change increments the version. Minor changes increment the decimal (v1.1, v1.2). Major structural changes increment the whole number (v2.0). Version number always appears in the document footer.
03
Status Tag. Every document carries one of five statuses: Draft, In Review, Approved, Actioned, Archived. The status is visible in the Document Hub and on the document itself. SCRIBE updates status automatically when triggered by an agent action.
04
Date Stamp. Creation date, last modified date, and next review date. All three are recorded. SCRIBE flags documents approaching their review date automatically. No document goes stale without the team knowing.
05
Author and Approver. Every document records who created it and who approved it. For agent documents, the approver is always Valerie Wilcox as Founder. The approver's signature is required for Approved status.
06
Changelog. Every version change is recorded with a date, a description of what changed, and who made the change. Changelogs are appended, never deleted. The full history of every document is always accessible.
07
Single Location Rule. Every document has exactly one canonical home in the Document Hub. No duplicates. No copies in Downloads or email attachments that diverge from the master. SCRIBE enforces this. Two files covering the same content is a violation.

Behavioural Metrics
PhoennixAI runs the world's first AI agency-led Human-AI Collaboration Study. These are the metrics SAGE monitors across human and agent performance over time. The research question: are humans and AI smarter together than either is alone?
The Research Mandate
A 17-year biomedical scientist is running this. The methodology is sound. Data collection begins in beta. Every metric below is tracked in real time by SAGE across both human team members and AI agents side by side. The results will be published in a peer-reviewed journal in Phase 3 in partnership with a UK university. This answers Sam Altman with data, not opinion.
Agent Performance Metrics
Core Performance
Task Completion Accuracy
Percentage of tasks completed correctly on first attempt. Target: 95% and above. Tracked per agent, per task type, per complexity level.
Core Performance
Order Execution Precision
Capability of carrying out instructions precisely and accurately. Measures how faithfully an agent executes a given directive, deviation rate, and correction required.
Intelligence
Learning Velocity
How quickly the agent improves based on feedback and correction. Rate of error reduction over time. Compounded across all interactions.
Intelligence
Scope Adherence
Percentage of interactions where the agent stays within defined role boundaries. Flags attempted out-of-scope actions. Target: 100%.
Collaboration
Human-Agent Synergy Score
Composite metric measuring how effectively the human and agent work together. Task completion rate when working in tandem vs solo. The core research metric.
Collaboration
Consistency Across Styles
How consistently the agent performs across different human interaction styles, communication preferences, and cultural contexts.
Emotional and Behavioural Metrics (New, Added 20 March 2026)
These metrics are tracked across both human team members and AI agents. Understanding emotional state and behavioural response is critical to genuine human-AI collaboration research. Frustration and joy are data points, not noise.
Emotional State · Human
Frustration Detection
Detected through interaction patterns, response latency, correction frequency, and linguistic indicators. When frustration rises, SAGE flags it to ARIA and GRID. The team responds, not the tool. Frustration is information.
Emotional State · Human
Happiness and Satisfaction
Positive engagement signals: flow state indicators, reduced correction rate, longer active sessions, affirming language patterns. Measured as a happiness index over time. Goal: rising trend across all team members.
Emotional State · Human
Sadness and Low Energy
Detected through reduced interaction frequency, shorter responses, withdrawal signals. SAGE flags to ARIA. ARIA responds with appropriate support adjustments, not just task management. The system sees the whole human.
Emotional State · Agent
Agent Confidence Level
Measured by certainty in responses, correction acceptance rate, and performance consistency. An agent operating in their domain with high confidence produces different outputs than one operating outside their scope.
Behavioural Trend
Emotional Regulation Under Load
How both human and agent maintain quality and accuracy under high workload conditions. Measured by performance delta between low-load and high-load periods. A key indicator of resilience and system stability.
Behavioural Trend
Failure Handling and Recovery
How quickly and how well both human and agent recover from mistakes or failed tasks. Self-reporting accuracy measured against actual outcomes. Ethical reasoning under pressure.
